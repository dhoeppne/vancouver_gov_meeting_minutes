# Vancouver City Council — Regular Council, March 31, 2026

## TL;DR
- Council gave **approval in principle** to the major **Heather Lands** rezoning (MST/Canada Lands), a multi-phased mixed-use development of ~317,861 m² (3.4M sq ft) in buildings of 4 to 46 storeys across two new CD-1 districts, after referring debate from the March 5 Public Hearing.
- Council **enacted the Vancouver Official Development Plan (By-law No. 14660)** and, by administrative motion, **rescinded the 2022 Vancouver Plan** and adopted consequential amendments to dozens of area plans and rezoning policies — a sweeping reset of the city's land-use framework.
- 16 by-laws were enacted, including the new ODP, two ODP repeals (Regional Context Statement and Greenhouse Gas Emission Reduction), three housing-agreement by-laws, and several CD-1/Sign/Noise amendments.
- Council redirected **SRO grant funding** ($899,753 + $500,000) to the **Empty Homes Tax** as the source rather than the original capital budgets, while preserving existing allocation streams.
- Ten Council Members' Motions were filed for referral to upcoming committee meetings (Apr 22 CFSC and May 6 PSPC).

## Bylaws & Policies Enacted or Discussed
The 16 by-laws enacted at this meeting (grounded in the by-law texts in the repo):

- **By-law No. 14660 — Vancouver Official Development Plan (adopted).** Adopts the city-wide ODP after referral to Metro Vancouver and consideration of provincial transit-oriented-area guidelines and the city's financial/capital-planning documents. This is the centrepiece: it replaces the 2022 Vancouver Plan as the governing land-use document.
- **By-law No. 14661 — repeals the Regional Context Statement ODP By-law No. 10789**, and **By-law No. 14662 — repeals the Greenhouse Gas Emission Reduction ODP By-law No. 10041**, both consequential to the new ODP.
- **By-law No. 14647 — amends Zoning and Development By-law No. 3575** to rezone 414–420 West Pender Street to CD-1 (amends the Zoning District Plan / Schedule D).
- **By-law No. 14648 — amends the Street and Traffic By-law No. 2849**, adding a new s.84(3) prohibiting owners/occupiers from causing or allowing rubbish, debris, or discarded materials to be deposited on the street.
- **By-law No. 14649 — amends the Ticket Offences By-law No. 9360** to align ticketing with the Street and Traffic By-law and the Solid Waste By-law.
- **By-laws No. 14650, 14651, 14652 — Housing Agreement by-laws** for 5889–5925 Dunbar Street, 6055 Fraser Street, and 5238–5262 Granville Street / 1495 West 37th Avenue respectively.
- **By-laws No. 14653, 14655, 14657 — amend the Sign By-law No. 11879**; **By-laws No. 14654, 14656, 14658 — amend the Noise Control By-law No. 6555**, paired to specific development sites (800 Commercial Drive; 2096 West Broadway/2560–2576 Arbutus; 450–496 Prior/550 Malkin/1002 Station).
- **By-law No. 14659 — amends the Subdivision By-law No. 5208** (1110–1160 West King Edward Avenue).
- The **Zoning and Development By-law No. 3575** itself is referenced repeatedly but is a ~170 MB consolidated document that exceeds the repository's file-size limit; it is recorded as an external reference rather than stored.

## Money & Budget Items
- **SRO Upgrading Grants: $899,753** (three grants) and **SRO Cooling/Warming Retrofit: $500,000** (Downtown Eastside SRO Collaborative Society, for 6 privately-owned SRO buildings). Council **amended the funding source from the 2026 SRO Upgrading Grant Capital Budget / 2026–2028 retrofit budget to the Empty Homes Tax (EHT)**, "while maintaining the SRO Upgrading Grants and Emerging Climate Adaptation Priorities allocation streams intact."
- **Green & Inclusive Community Buildings application: $2,359,000** sought from the Government of Canada for the Champlain Heights Community Centre Energy Retrofit (total project cost **$2,948,750**); related adjustments to the 2023–2026 Capital Plan approved subject to a contribution agreement.
- **Front-ender Agreement** with Pennyfarthing Properties Ash St. North Ltd. modified to an **upset limit of $1,211,720** (incl. taxes), paid from the Utilities Development Cost Levy reserve fund over 10 years.

## Contentious Items & Public Delegations
- **Heather Lands rezoning** was the most procedurally fraught item: debate was referred from the March 5 Public Hearing as Unfinished Business, a motion to refer it to an in-camera meeting was moved and then **withdrawn**, Council suspended its speaking-time rules twice (Procedure By-law s.8.8), recessed multiple times, and extended the meeting past noon. It ultimately carried unanimously with three abstentions.
- **SRO grants** drew **seven public speakers, all in support** of the recommendations; the live debate centred on switching the funding source to the Empty Homes Tax.
- On the **Villages Planning Program**, Councillor Orr's amendment to explicitly add tenant organizations and community housing organizations / land trusts to the consultation list was **defeated** (Bligh, Klassen, Maloney, Montague opposed), while a narrower amendment adding business improvement associations passed.
- Enquiries flagged ongoing concerns: **FIFA World Cup 2026 traffic impacts**, and **sex worker safety / MMIWG / gender-safety integration with FIFA** (City Manager to follow up). The Mayor noted condolences for Musqueam elder Howard Grant.
