(b) after section 3.2.1, adds a new section 3.2.2 as follows:
“3.2.2 Despite section 3.2.1 above, where the portion of the residential floor area
to be allocated to social housing results in:
(a) a single social housing dwelling unit, or
(b) a single residential unit associated with and forming an integral part
of an artist studio in the IC-3 district,
that dwelling unit must be occupied by a household with an income below
housing income limits as set out in the current “Housing Income Limits” table
published by the British Columbia Housing Management Commission, or
equivalent publication and the maximum rent must not exceed a rate that is
equivalent to 30% of the gross income of the household occupying the
dwelling unit, except that if the unit is rented at the shelter component of
income assistance, the maximum rent is the shelter component of income
assistance.”;
(c) after section 7.2, adds a new section 8 as follows:
“8 CASH IN LIEU
8.1 Payment of Money Instead of Providing Social Housing
8.1.1 Where indicated in the applicable district schedule, as an alternative to the
provision of social housing within a development, a cash in lieu payment
may be provided at the applicable zoning district rate on the proportion of
residential floor area that is otherwise required to be developed as social
housing:
Zoning District Cash In Lieu Rate
FC-2 (Sub-Area E) $6,558 per m2
FSD $5,920 per m2
R1-1 $5,920 per m2RM-8A $5,920 per m2
8.1.2 The cash in lieu rates in section 8.1.1 above are estimated capital costs
associated with developing the required social housing:
(a) including planning, professional services, financing and
construction costs;
(b) excluding costs associated with the creation of air space parcels
and certain site-wide costs such as land acquisition, demolition and
servicing; and
(c) determined after consideration of a financial feasibility analysis and
anticipated development viability implications.
8.1.3 A cash in lieu payment must be made prior to the issuance of a building
permit that authorizes development which would otherwise be subject to a
requirement to provide social housing.
8.1.4 Despite section 8.1.3 above, where there is a change in tenure or other
development that does not require a building permit, a cash in lieu payment
must be made prior to the issuance of a development permit that
authorizes the development which would otherwise be subject to a
requirement to provide social housing.
8.1.5 If a building permit is issued in stages, a cash in lieu payment must be
made prior to the issuance of the first building permit.
8.1.6 If a development takes place in stages authorized by separate building
permits, a cash in lieu payment must be made with respect to each building
permit that would otherwise be subject to a requirement to provide a
portion of the residential floor area as social housing.”.
