BARD ON THE BEACH
New Multi-Year Agreement
Framework for Main Festival
Site at Vanier Park
Park Board Committee Meeting
Monday, May 25, 2026PURPOSE/OBJECTIVES
▪ Present a new opportunity
involving the exploration of a
multi-year agreement for BOTB
▪ Review the needs and benefits
of this initiative
▪ Seek the Board’s approval to
explore the opportunity for a
period of up to 10 years
2BACKGROUND | Bard on the Beach
▪ Established in 1990, BOTB is
Western Canada’s largest not-
for-profit Shakespeare Festival
▪ Presented in Vanier Park
annually
▪ Offers Shakespeare plays,
related dramas, and special
events from June to September
▪ BOTB educational division offers
year-round program opportunities
3BACKGROUND | Bard on the Beach
▪ Performing arts sector continues to
face significant post-pandemic
pressures; BOTB navigating
challenging financial reality
▪ Board recently approved:
• Pilot program (2024)
• Burrard Marina space lease (2026)
▪ Continued focus on reducing
repeatable structural costs
▪ New opportunity identified; long-
term agreement
4PROPOSAL
▪ Seeking Board approval to
explore a broader more
coordinated strategy
▪ Enable additional investments to
enhance park usability and allow
additional physical assets to
remain in the park year-round
▪ Assets would not be permanent;
would remain removable
5PROPOSAL
▪ Goals/objectives:
• Reduce repeated annual construction
and dismantling activities
• Minimize environmental impacts
• Lower long-term operating costs
• Improve park usability, accessibility
and public enjoyment
▪ Longer-term planning framework (up
to 10 years) required to enable
investment, cost reductions, and
operational efficiencies
▪ Lease certainty is essential to
amortize needed investments
6PROPOSAL
▪ Potential investments to be
investigated include:
• Winterizing and retaining the
Mainstage tent year-round
• Adding anchor points for the smaller
theatre tent
• Upgrading gravel pathways for year-
round use
• Exploring a year-round marquee or
covered gathering structure
• Reimagining Bard’s main entranceway
• Investigating washroom trailer
solutions
7BENEFITS
▪ Reduce repeated construction and
dismantling activities
▪ Reduced environmental impacts
▪ Enable long-term cost efficiencies
▪ Support a stronger, more resilient
cultural partner within Vanier Park
▪ Improve park amenities, accessibility,
and overall experience
▪ Enhance year-round park activation
and programming
▪ Generate incremental revenues for
Park Board
8KEY CONSIDERATIONS
▪ Financial
• Initiative will require investment in staff
time; no incremental costs to Park
Board
▪ Engagement
• Internal/external stakeholders
• MST rightsholders
▪ Issues/risks
• Will need to navigate a potential longer-
term agreement and the broader Vanier
Park planning initiative
• Potential community feedback
9SUMMARY
▪ BOTB is a longstanding cultural
partner
▪ Facing ongoing and challenging
financial pressures
▪ New opportunity has been identified
to support the Festival through the
potential development of a long-term
agreement of up to 10 years
▪ Staff have considered the many
aspects of this application; confident it
can be implemented successfully
10RECOMMENDATIONS
A. THAT the Vancouver Board of Parks and Recreation (“Park Board”) direct
staff to explore the development of a new multi-year agreement framework
for Bard on the Beach at their main festival location in Vanier Park;
B. THAT, subject to a positive assessment, the Park Board direct staff to
present the proposed terms of a new agreement framework to the Board at
the earliest possible date; and
C. THAT no legal rights or obligations shall arise, and no consents,
permissions, or licenses are granted hereby, and none shall arise or be
granted hereafter unless and until all contemplated legal documentation
has been executed and delivered by all parties.
