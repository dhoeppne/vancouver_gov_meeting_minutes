MOTION on NOTICE
Submitted at the Regular Park Board Meeting on May 4, 2026
Recognition of Jim Clive and Howard Kelsey for Their Contributions to Sport and
Community at Kitsilano Beach Park
SUBMITTED BY: Commissioner Jensen
WHEREAS
1. Jim Clive has been officially confirmed for induction into the BC Sports Hall of Fame, Class
of 2026, recognizing over five decades of leadership, coaching, and volunteer service that
helped define beach volleyball in British Columbia;
2. Jim Clive is widely regarded as a founding builder of beach volleyball in Vancouver, having
established the first permanent public volleyball courts at Kitsilano Beach, founded the
Kits Beach Volleyball Association, and hosted numerous community and provincial level
tournaments that have fostered inclusive recreation for thousands of participants of all
ages;
3. Through his advocacy and mentorship, Jim Clive advanced equitable access to sport, built
partnerships among recreation groups, and helped make Kits Beach a welcoming
destination for active living and community connection;
4. Howard Kelsey, a world class Canadian basketball player and sport leader, represented
Team Canada (1977 – 1988) for 11 years, winning gold medals at the Commonwealth
Basketball Championships (1978) and the FISU World University Games (1983), and
competing as a two-time Olympian (1980 and 1984) with a 4th place finish at the 1984
Olympic Games;
5. Howard Kelsey’s continued service to athlete development through Basketball British
Columbia, Canada Basketball, and his ongoing mentorship of youth has strengthened
participation and leadership in Vancouver and across Canada;
6. He has been recognized by induction into the Canadian Basketball Hall of Fame (2019),
the Basketball British Columbia Hall of Fame, the Point Grey Secondary School Hall
of Fame, and through the Governor General of Canada’s Medallion for Extraordinary
International Contributions to Canada;
7. Kitsilano Park already honours local and national excellence in sport with the naming of
the Vasek Pospisil Tennis Court reflecting the Vancouver Park Board’s commitment to
celebrating athletic achievement and community building within public recreation
spaces; and
8. Recognizing Jim Clive and Howard Kelsey at their respective facilities would celebrate
their enduring legacies of teamwork, mentorship, and civic pride at Kitsilano Beach Park.
Park Board Meeting: May 25, 2026THEREFORE BE IT RESOLVED
A. THAT The Vancouver Park Board formally acknowledge and honour the contributions of
Jim Clive and Howard Kelsey to Kitsilano Beach Park and the broader Vancouver
recreation system;
B. THAT staff be directed to report back with options to commemorate Jim Clive and Howard
Kelsey legacy within the Kitsilano Beach Park project, including:
i. Consideration of naming the beach volleyball court at Kitsilano Beach Park in Jim
Clive's honour and a basketball court at Kitsilano Beach Park, consistent with Park
Board naming policies;
ii. Other appropriate recognition measures, including plaques, dedications, or
naming of park features; and
C. THAT Staff report back in a timely manner to enable the Board to consider and implement
recognition without delay.
Park Board Meeting: May 25, 2026
