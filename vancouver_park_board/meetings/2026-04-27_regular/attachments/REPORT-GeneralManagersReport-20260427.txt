The following report is intended to provide an overview and update on recent and upcoming activities within
the Park Board.
Memorial West Park Improvements
At Memorial West Park, the hydraulic irrigation system installed in 1980 had reached end of life
and was no longer operational. This system was one of nine remaining hydraulic installations
across the parks network prioritized for replacement. A complete irrigation system replacement
has now been completed on this heavily used Class B-1, sand based, multi-use sports field.
System design and construction were delivered internally by the Park Board Irrigation Team in
collaboration with Park Operations staff.
Over a three-week construction period, more than 4,800 linear feet of irrigation piping, along with
wiring, control boxes, and irrigation heads, were installed. Construction concluded in early
February. Despite challenging environmental conditions, Park Operations teams maintained the
project schedule to ensure the field reopened by April 1, in time for the 2026 Little League season.
Key benefits of the new irrigation system include targeted water delivery improves efficiency and
delivers significant water use savings, improved field conditions with fewer dry spots and reduced
water pooling, remote system control capability enables additional operational and water savings,
simplified maintenance and troubleshooting, reducing labour and response time, and enhanced
safety, playability, consistency, and overall field quality.
Upcoming Sports Field Projects include renovations to the East ball field at China Creek North,
the enclosed girls’ softball diamond at Trafalgar Park, and both North little league fields at
Nanaimo Park.
Inclusive Playground Design – Public Engagement Launch
The Inclusive Playground Design Project advances Board direction to improve accessibility and
inclusion in Vancouver’s parks. The project includes the City’s first Inclusive Destination
Playground at Memorial South Park in the Sunset neighbourhood, as well as the development of
Inclusive Playground Design Guidelines to support future playground renewals city wide.
Memorial South Park was recommended as the first of the five destination play areas based on
its accessibility, transit access, existing park amenities, and location within an equity initiative
zone.
Public engagement will take place from May 5 to May 26, focusing on inclusive play principles
and early outreach to key interest holders and community partners. Engagement will include a
Shape Your City webpage and online survey, targeted outreach to inclusivity organizations, and
an on site presence during the Memorial South Park Centennial event, hosted by South
Vancouver Neighbourhood House, on May 24 from 11:00 a.m. to 1:00 p.m. Feedback from this
engagement will inform the development of the Memorial South concept design and draft Inclusive
Play Design Guidelines, which will be shared with the public through a second round of
engagement anticipated for Q4 2026, prior to returning to the Board for review and decision in
2027.

Vancouver’s Community Centre Associations deliver a wide range of special events throughout
the year, celebrating holidays, culture, music, and community connection. These events are
typically offered at little to no cost, ensuring that families have access to inclusive, engaging
activities within their neighbourhoods.
Over the recent Easter weekend, community centres across Vancouver hosted a variety of
activities, including egg hunts, games, crafts, bouncy castles, and face painting. This year alone,
more than 2,050 children participated in egg hunts citywide.
Other annual events like Breakfast with Santa welcomed over 4,100 families, while during
Halloween, approximately 3,000 children had a safe space to trick-or-treat and enjoy themed
activities such as parties and haunted houses. Celebrations for Lunar New Year, Diwali, and
Family Day also attract thousands of participants annually, reflecting the diverse and vibrant
communities these centres serve.
Community Centre Associations also offer centre specific events such as outdoor movie nights,
music in the park, community festivals, Mother’s Day celebrations, and summer kick-off events.
The continued success of these events is made possible through the dedication of the
Associations and volunteers, Youth Council members, and the ongoing support of Park Board
staff. Their collective efforts are integral to delivering welcoming, accessible, and high-quality
experiences that foster connection and community well-being across Vancouver.
Community Youth Development 2025 Highlights
Community Centre Associations (CCAs) and Park Board continue to work collaboratively together
to provide engagement opportunities for youth across the city. CCAs provide programmatic
direction, support, and funding at the local level. Citywide, Park Board provides the Community
Youth Development Coordinator role, and 19 Community Youth Worker positions who oversee
the delivery of programs and services, and partner with other youth serving agencies to
strengthen a broader system of youth supports.
In 2025, CCAs delivered over 12,000 hours of open gym time and nearly 19,000 hours in games
rooms, creating safe and welcoming spaces where youth can stay active, build relationships, and
feel connected to their community. Youth also contributed nearly 16,000 volunteer hours,
demonstrating strong leadership, commitment, and a willingness to give back to their
communities.
Additional 2025 highlights include:
− the Youth Awards held at the Roundhouse Arts, Culture and Community Centre, where
over 20 youth were recognized for leadership, resilience, and achievement;
− Camp Sasamat, which provided more than 80 youth with overnight outdoor experiences,
many for the first time;
− City-Wide Youth Council, which continues to build civic engagement and leadership skills,
including organizing a large-scale clothing drive that filled a five-ton truck for the Hastings
Project.
