4Advancing the Urban Forest Strategy
▪ Park Board adopted the Urban Forest Strategy on May 12, 2025.
▪ The Urban Forest Strategy identifies five primary goals - updating the Tree
Inspection Policy (1993) relates to Goal #1:
• Goal #1: Manage trees for health and safety;
• Goal #2: Protect the urban forest;
• Goal #3: Engage residents in the urban forest;
• Goal #4: Monitor the status and condition of the urban forest; and
• Goal #5: Expand the urban forest
▪ To support the long-term operationalization of the policy, Urban Forestry will also
develop an 'Urban Forest Management Standards' document in 2027/2028

7What the Policy Is Designed to Do
▪ The new Tree Inspection Policy will replace the existing policy from 1993
▪ Establishes a consistent policy for all City and Park Board trees with one department (Urban Forestry)
tasked to implement
▪ Defines:
• Tree categories
• Inspection frequencies and methods
- Level 1: visual inspection, conducted from a particular vantage point
- Level 2: a 360-degree ground-based inspection
• Risk-based and time-sensitive response requirements
• Aligns with recognized professional standards (ISA / TRAQ)
