DESIGNATED SMOKING
AREAS AT SELECT
SPECIAL EVENTS IN
SUMMER 2026
Pilot Program
Park Board Committee Meeting
June 1, 2026PURPOSE/OBJECTIVES
▪ Present concept of designated
smoking areas at events to the
Park Board
▪ Review requests from two event
organizers (Trixstar Productions
and Feaster Festivals & Events)
to implement pilot program in
Summer 2026
▪ Seek the Board’s approval for
this request
2BACKGROUND | Parks Control Bylaw
▪ No smoking policy approved by
the Park Board on July 5, 2010
▪ Regulation applies to all
Vancouver parks and beaches
▪ Aligned to the Park Board’s
commitment to promote healthy
urban environment and support
active living and wellness
▪ Followed the introduction of
similar smoking restrictions other
North American cities
3BACKGROUND | Special Events
▪ Special events play an essential
role in building community
connection, diversity and cohesion,
and driving economic activity
▪ Park Board supports over 1,500
events and other activities across
its network
▪ Staff work collaboratively with event
organizers to enable events,
improve operations, and
minimize/reduce potential risks
4BACKGROUND | What’s the Issue?
▪ Despite significant efforts, illegal
smoking at events is frequent at
large public gatherings
▪ Surveys indicate that up to 37% of
festival-goers smoke, leading to
routine rule-breaking at non-
smoking events
▪ Attendees exploit oversight/
enforcement gaps to circumvent
smoking bans
5PROPOSAL
▪ Trixstar Productions (TP) and
Feaster Festivals & Events (FFE)
seeking an exemption to the Park
Board’s no smoking policy to offer
designated smoking areas, on a
pilot program basis, for three
Summer 2026 events:
• TP – Great Outdoor Comedy Festival;
Stanley Park (19+)
• FFE – Country Club & Rosé Disco
Festivals; Jericho Beach Park (19+)
6PROPOSAL
▪ Objective is to reduce illegal
smoking at their events, reduce
public safety risks, and enhance
park protection
▪ If approved, staff would work with
organizers on the detailed
planning and implementation
(location, signage, fencing,
scrimming, staffing, no F&B, etc.)
7WHY IS THIS NEEDED?
▪ Designated smoking areas at events help
reduce illicit smoking and improve public
safety and enhance park protection
▪ Organizers have consistently observed
that offering clearly delineated smoking
zones decreases illicit smoking
throughout the broader venue
▪ Pairing prohibitions with designated
smoking areas has been shown to
promote significantly better overall
compliance
▪ Accordingly, designated smoking areas
for events have been introduced in cities
across Canada
8PAST PRECEDENTS
▪ Park Bylaws restrict consumption of
liquor in park spaces, unless
approved by the Board
▪ Park Board regularly approves
designated areas for liquor
consumption in parks
▪ Proposed pilot program seeks to
follow a similar model
▪ Designated smoking areas would be
expressly approved by the Board,
while maintaining the integrity and
authority of the no smoking policy
9BENEFITS
▪ Reduced illegal
smoking/improved Park Bylaw
compliance
▪ Improved public safety
▪ Improved park protection during
the dry summer season
▪ Operational efficiencies for event
organizers
▪ Improved guest experience
10KEY CONSIDERATIONS
▪ Financial – all expenses to be borne by
event producers; no costs to Park Board
▪ FEST Committee support – pilot
program endorsed by all major delivery
partners/agencies
▪ Data collection – to measure success
and support report back to Board
▪ Review/report back – before potential
introduction at future events
▪ Risks/challenges – potential reputational
risks and/or misinterpretation about
program objectives; mitigate through
communications
11SUMMARY
▪ Illicit smoking at large events
continues to be an issue of concern
▪ Designated smoking areas have
proven effective and are being
introduced in cities across Canada
▪ Report contemplates the introduction
of designated smoking areas for three
events on a pilot program basis
▪ Staff confident that this initiative can
be delivered successfully and
recommend the approval of this pilot
program
12RECOMMENDATIONS
A. THAT the Vancouver Park Board approve, as a pilot program, the
requirements of the Park Board Smoking Regulation Bylaw not be strictly
enforced in relation to the 2026 Great Outdoor Comedy Festival and the
2026 Rosé Disco and Country Club events to allow for the establishment of
designated smoking areas at these events; and
B. THAT, Park Board staff will report back to the Board regarding the efficacy
of this pilot project no later than March 31, 2027.
