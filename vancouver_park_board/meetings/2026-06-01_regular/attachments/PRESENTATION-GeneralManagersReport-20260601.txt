3Hadden Park Floating Sauna Update
▪ A proposed rezoning of four Custody
Care & Maintenance parcels in Hadden
Park will go to Council on June 2, 2026. If
approved for referral, Council will decide
on the rezoning at a future public
hearing.
▪ By adding ‘Beauty and Wellness Centre’
as an allowable land use, the rezoning
would enable a proposal for a floating
sauna to move forward.
▪ The sauna facility would be managed
through a sub-lease from the Vancouver
Maritime Museum (VMM).VMM lease
updates to facilitate the sub-lease will be
brought to Council at a future date, and
Park Board staff will be involved
in finalized lease terms that relate to
parkland.

5Council Items related to Parks & Recreation (1/2)
▪ Council to receive a report on June 2, 2026, titled “Vision Zero Safe Mobility Plan”, which would
advance the City’s commitment to Vision Zero and the goal to eliminate all traffic fatalities and serious
injuries from our transportation network
▪ Council to receive a report on June 2, 2026, titled “Proposed Amendments to the Election Bylaw”,
which would align the Election Bylaw with the Province’s amendments to the Vancouver Charter,
change the processing of mail in ballots, and implement additional Special Voting Opportunities to be
incorporated into the 2026 municipal election.
▪ Council to receive a report on June 2, 2026, titled “2026 Cultural Grants (Projects, Cultural Spaces,
Capacity) and SRO Upgrading Grant”, which would approve a total of $2.6M in grants to 118 non-
profit organizations to support programs and services for artists, the cultural community, and the
public.
▪ Council to receive a report on June 2, 2026, titled “Auditor General Committee Recommendations
Transmittal Report”, which would endorse the ten recommendations of the Auditor General’s Report
entitled “Audit of Community Grants”.
▪ Council to receive a report on June 3, 2026, titled “Downtown Eastside (DTES) Plan Implementation
Grant Program Allocation 2026”, which would approve a total of $610,051 to 17 non-profit agencies to
