MOTION on NOTICE
Submitted at the Regular Park Board Meeting on May 25, 2026
Beach Safety and Marine Protection
SUBMITTED BY: Commissioner Bastyovanszky
WHEREAS
1. Vancouver’s beaches and waterfront parks support significant recreational swimming and
water access, particularly at Sunset Beach and English Bay;
2. English Bay, False Creek, Spanish Banks and Stanley Park waters are increasingly
shared by swimmers, paddlers, recreational boating, tourism activity, and marine wildlife
including whales;
3. Vancouver’s urban waterfront has become significantly more crowded and complex, while
recreational marine safety awareness and infrastructure have not kept pace;
4. Sunset Beach and Spanish Banks represent some of the closest points of interaction
between swimmers, paddlers, high-speed personal watercraft, and recreational boating
traffic within Vancouver’s urban waterfront;
5. A recent incident involving a personal high-speed watercraft striking a whale near Stanley
Park heightened public concern regarding swimmer safety, marine safety, and marine
wildlife protection;
6. Existing marine protection efforts in Vancouver have largely focused on commercial
shipping activity and port operations, while comparatively less attention has been given to
recreational vessel interactions in heavily used urban swimming areas;
7. Vancouver’s marine wildlife and waterfront recreation are important components of the
city’s tourism economy and international reputation;
8. Responsible marine operators have demonstrated that technologies such as geo-fencing,
automatic speed reduction systems, and clearly defined operating boundaries can support
improved marine safety outcomes;
9. Abandoned, grounded, or derelict vessels periodically affect Vancouver beaches and
nearshore recreational waters, creating environmental, navigational, and public safety
concerns; and
10. Authority over marine navigation and vessel regulation primarily rests with the
Government of Canada, including Transport Canada and Fisheries and Oceans Canada,
together with the Vancouver Fraser Port Authority and Vancouver Police Department
Marine Unit;
Park Board Meeting: June 1, 2026THEREFORE BE IT RESOLVED
A. THAT the Vancouver Board of Parks and Recreation request that Transport Canada,
Fisheries and Oceans Canada, the Vancouver Fraser Port Authority, the Vancouver Police
Department Marine Unit, and other relevant agencies review opportunities to improve
swimmer safety, recreational marine safety, and marine wildlife protection adjacent to
Vancouver beaches and park waters;
B. THAT the Board advocate for:
i. enhanced swim safety buoys and protected swimming boundary infrastructure
adjacent to Vancouver beaches, including Sunset Beach and Spanish Banks as
areas of priority;
ii. expanded low-speed or no-wake measures adjacent to heavily used swimming
beaches and recreational waterfront areas;
iii. clear and standardized public guidance regarding minimum distances from
swimming beaches and whales, including consideration of 300m setbacks from
swimming beaches and 400m setbacks from whales for high-speed personal
watercraft;
iv. improved public signage, maps, and awareness information regarding whale
protection, swimming areas, low-speed zones, and recreational marine safety at
beaches, marinas, seawalls, and vessel launch locations;
v. review of recreational vessel operator education, certification, and safety
awareness measures relating to marine wildlife protection and urban recreational
waterways;
vi. consideration of whether additional operating conditions, exclusion areas, or
restrictions are appropriate for high-speed personal watercraft in heavily used
urban recreational waters; and
vii. improved interagency coordination, prevention, and timely response regarding
abandoned, grounded, or derelict vessels affecting Vancouver beaches and
nearshore recreational waters;
C. THAT the Vancouver Board of Parks and Recreation express its willingness to support the
placement of marine safety signage, maps, and whale awareness information at
appropriate beaches, parks, seawalls, and waterfront access points under Park Board
jurisdiction; and
D. THAT the Chair of the Vancouver Board of Parks and Recreation communicate this
request to the relevant federal, port, and marine authorities on behalf of the Board.
