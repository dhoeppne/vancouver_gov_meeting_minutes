MOTION on NOTICE
Submitted at the Regular Park Board Meeting on May 25, 2026
Think Accessible Play: Advancing Vancouver’s Second Destination Inclusive Sensory
and All-Abilities Park with a Communication Board Pilot at Queen Elizabeth Park
SUBMITTED BY: Commissioner Virdi
WHEREAS
1. Queen Elizabeth Park is Vancouver’s highest point and one of the city’s premier
destination parks, attracting residents and visitors from across the region and
internationally;
2. Queen Elizabeth Park contains significant citywide amenities, including Bloedel
Conservatory, the Quarry Garden, restaurants, event spaces, and extensive walking
paths, but does not currently include a destination playground designed to serve children
of varying physical, cognitive, and sensory abilities;
3. The Vancouver Board of Parks and Recreation has identified accessibility, inclusion, and
equitable access to play as important priorities, and is advancing Vancouver’s first
destination inclusive sensory play space;
4. Queen Elizabeth Park presents an opportunity to establish Vancouver’s second
destination inclusive sensory all-abilities park within one of Vancouver’s most significant
civic parks;
5. The Board has approved new destination attractions at Queen Elizabeth Park, including
a proposed zipline and tree canopy walk, reinforcing the park’s evolving role as a citywide
destination and creating an opportunity to advance complementary family-oriented public
amenities;
6. VanPlay, through Initiative P.1.3, identifies the design and construction of five additional
fully accessible destination play areas throughout the city, in addition to the ongoing
integration of accessible play features in parks, reflecting the Park Board’s commitment to
ensuring parks and recreation spaces welcome everyone and provide equitable access to
inclusive experiences for residents of all ages and abilities; and
7. The Vancouver Board of Parks and Recreation’s 2023–2026 Capital Plan includes funding
for a new playground at Queen Elizabeth Park, providing an existing capital framework
through which this work may be advanced;
8. Universal communication access is a key component of truly inclusive public space,
including for non-verbal individuals, educators, multilingual families, young children, and
individuals with diverse communication needs, and can be supported through the use of
accessible communication boards and inclusive learning tools in park environments.
Park Board Meeting: June 1, 2026THEREFORE BE IT RESOLVED
A. THAT the Vancouver Board of Parks and Recreation direct staff to undertake planning
and feasibility work, within available funding in the approved 2023–2026 Capital Plan, for
the development of Vancouver’s second destination sensory all-abilities park at Queen
Elizabeth Park;
B. THAT in undertaking this work, staff be directed to consider how the proposed inclusive
sensory all-abilities park may complement existing, approved, and emerging destination
amenities within Queen Elizabeth Park, including the proposed zipline and tree canopy
walk;
C. THAT staff be directed to identify and evaluate potential locations within Queen Elizabeth
Park, giving preference to sites that:
i. provide convenient access for children, families, and caregivers of varying mobility
levels;
ii. are in close proximity to public washroom facilities;
iii. are in close proximity to parking, including accessible parking;
iv. minimize impacts on significant horticultural, heritage, and viewscape features;
and
v. can support a destination-quality play experience appropriate to the park’s citywide
role, including consideration of nature-based and context-sensitive design
approaches aligned with the park’s character;
D. THAT staff be directed to undertake public and stakeholder engagement to inform both
the preferred location and future play features of the proposed sensory all-abilities park,
including engagement with families, park users, accessibility advocates, community
organizations, and relevant advisory groups;
E. THAT staff be directed to consider inclusive, destination-quality play features, including
but not limited to accessible play components, sensory-rich play experiences, quiet or
retreat spaces, accessible surfacing and pathways, inclusive swings, social play elements,
and nature-integrated play opportunities appropriate to the Queen Elizabeth Park setting,
and to incorporate universal communication access features as part of a pilot approach,
including accessible communication boards designed to support non-verbal individuals,
educators, multilingual families, and individuals with diverse communication needs; and
F. THAT staff be directed to explore the implementation of accessible communication boards
as a pilot initiative within the proposed park, in consultation with relevant experts and
organizations including Forever Kind Learning Ltd. and others working in this field, to
assess best practices for inclusive communication tools in public parks, including but not
limited to Picture Assisted Communication (PAC), American Sign Language (ASL)
supports, Braille integration, color-coded learning systems, and interactive communication
features designed to foster accessibility and inclusion; and
Park Board Meeting: June 1, 2026G. THAT staff report back to the Board via memo by July 27, 2026, on timelines and
preliminary concept directions for both communication boards and inclusive play features
for the proposed park, including potential approaches to implementing accessible
communication boards as a pilot initiative within Queen Elizabeth Park.
