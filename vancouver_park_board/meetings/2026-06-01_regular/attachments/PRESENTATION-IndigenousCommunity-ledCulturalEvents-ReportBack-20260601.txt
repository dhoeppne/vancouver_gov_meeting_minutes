3Background
▪ In 2022 Park Board approved $150,000 from the Corporate
Sponsorship Fund to support emergent Indigenous community-led
cultural ceremony and events in parks.
▪ Between 2022-2025, Park Board supported 18 cultural events in
parks led by the local Nations and Urban Indigenous organisations.
▪ The one-time funding enabled staff to bring operational,
technical, production and funding support to the organization of
Indigenous-led cultural events and ceremonies in parks.

10Corporate Sponsorship Fund (CSF)
▪ Park Board’s policy for use of CSF was developed in 1998 and
includes the following use criteria:
a) To fund one-time expenditures to a limit of $50,000 that will support revenue-
generating activities and the money repaid into the Corporate Sponsorship Account
from the increased revenues within a period of three years
b) To fund initiatives with one-time expenditures to a limit of $50,000 that will result in
improved efficiencies
c) To fund one-time expenditures over and above the existing funding levels in the
operating budget which are beneficial to the overall Parks and Recreation system
▪ All contributions from the CSF require Board approval
11Corporate Sponsorship Fund (CSF)
This work has been funded once through the Corporate Sponsorship
Fund (CSF). Given its importance Staff support this 2nd funding request for
the following reasons:
• Request aligns with approved Fund use Criteria C
• The importance of this ongoing work to our Reconciliation Mandate
and continuing to build positive relationships of trust with the local
Nations and Urban Indigenous Community
• CSF has a healthy balance which can be used to support this
important work
