MOTION on NOTICE
Submitted at the Regular Park Board Meeting on May 25, 2026
Prohibition and Restriction of Motorized Personal Watercraft in Burrard Inlet
SUBMITTED BY: Commissioner Digby
WHEREAS
1. The mandate of the Vancouver Board of Parks and Recreation includes the protection of
shoreline ecosystems, marine biodiversity, public beaches, and safe recreational access to
Vancouver’s waterfront;
2. Burrard Inlet has experienced a notable increase in sightings of cetaceans and marine wildlife
in recent years, including orca, humpback, and grey whales, reflecting both ecological
recovery efforts and the growing environmental significance of local marine habitat;
3. Scientific and public concern continues to grow regarding the impacts of high-speed
motorized personal watercraft, including Jet Skis and Sea Doos, on marine mammals and
coastal ecosystems through noise pollution, wake disturbance, shoreline intrusion, and the
heightened risk of wildlife harassment or collision;
4. A widely publicized whale strike incident in British Columbia waters on May 4, 2026
intensified public awareness regarding the risks posed by fast-moving personal watercraft
operating in sensitive marine environments;
5. Personal watercraft are uniquely capable of operating at high speeds in shallow near-shore
waters, intertidal zones, and ecologically sensitive areas that are increasingly being
prioritized for biodiversity restoration, eelgrass protection, and habitat enhancement
initiatives;
6. Vancouver’s beaches and waterfront parks are among the city’s most important public
ecological and recreational assets, and growing public concern has been expressed
regarding excessive noise, unsafe operation, and environmental disturbance associated with
personal watercraft activity near swimming beaches and shoreline parks;
7. Other jurisdictions, including Sydney, Washington State, Hawaii, Toronto, and Quebec have
implemented motorized personal watercraft exclusion zones, speed restrictions, and/or
enhanced enforcement measures to protect whales and/or improve public safety; and
8. The Park Board’s existing 2019 approved “On Water” policy primarily addresses non-
motorized recreational uses and does not currently provide policy direction or advocacy
guidance respecting the environmental and safety impacts of motorized personal watercraft
in Burrard Inlet;
Park Board Meeting: June 1, 2026THEREFORE BE IT RESOLVED
A. THAT The Vancouver Board of Parks and Recreation formally adopt a policy position
advocating for the prohibition or significant restriction of high-speed recreational motorized
personal watercraft, including Jet Skis and Sea Doos, within Burrard Inlet and adjacent
near-shore waters surrounding Vancouver parks and beaches;
B. Park Board staff be directed to report back on measures available within Park Board
jurisdiction to discourage or restrict the launching, staging, and operation of motorized
personal watercraft from Park Board-managed facilities, including signage, public
education, launch conditions, and potential amendments to relevant policies or permits;
C. The Chair of the Park Board be directed to advocate to relevant federal and regional
authorities, including Transport Canada, Fisheries and Oceans Canada, Vancouver
Fraser Port Authority, and the Vancouver Police Department Marine Unit, for stronger
regulation of motorized personal watercraft activity in Burrard Inlet in order to improve
marine wildlife protection, reduce shoreline disturbance, and enhance public safety; and
D. The Park Board further call upon senior governments and marine regulatory agencies to
prioritize Burrard Inlet as an urban marine conservation and stewardship zone,
recognizing its growing importance for marine biodiversity, climate resilience, and public
ecological education.
Park Board Meeting: June 1, 2026
