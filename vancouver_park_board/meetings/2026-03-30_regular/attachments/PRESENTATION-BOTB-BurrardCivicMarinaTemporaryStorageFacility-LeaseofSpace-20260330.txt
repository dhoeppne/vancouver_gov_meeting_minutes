▪ Present new opportunity involving

▪ Review the needs and benefits of

▪ Seek the Board’s approval to

2BACKGROUND | Bard on the Beach
▪ Established in 1990, BOTB is
Western Canada’s largest not-for-
profit Shakespeare Festival
▪ Presented in Vanier Park annually
▪ Offers Shakespeare plays, related
dramas, and special events from
June to September
▪ BOTB educational division offers
year-round program opportunities
3BACKGROUND | Bard on the Beach
▪ Several challenges have emerged
over the Festival’s 35+ year history;
created both financial and
operational pressures
▪ BOTB and Park Board staff have
worked collaboratively to explore
solutions for these challenges
▪ Pilot program successfully
approved by Board in 2024
▪ Second opportunity now identified
with potential meaningful benefits
4PROPOSAL
▪ BOTB is seeking to lease
approximately 20,000 square feet
at Burrard Marina to create a
temporary storage facility
(Quonset tent structure)
▪ Requested space is located in
marina land storage lot; staff
determined space can be made
available without significant
impacts to marina operations
▪ 10-year term required to
offset/recover investment
5WHY IS THIS NEEDED?
▪ Post-pandemic cost increases are
having major impact on financial
viability of arts/culture events
▪ Forced BOTB to review every
aspect of their operations
▪ Current warehouse lease ends in
December 2026; market rates for
traditional warehouse space are
unviable
▪ Innovative solution needed; meant
creating their own storage facility

8BENEFITS
▪ Supports a more efficient
medium-term storage approach
▪ Enables important cost reduction
opportunities for BOTB
▪ Delivers environmental and
neighbourhood impacts
▪ Protects Park Board revenue
streams; annual inflation escalator
▪ Provides potential administrative
efficiencies
9KEY CONSIDERATIONS
▪ Financial
• All expenses to be born by BOTB; no
costs to Park Board
• Park Board revenues fully maintained and
protected
• Revenues will flow to marina operations
▪ Risks
• Initiative considered low risk; minimal
impact on marina operations
• Anticipate mitigations for any potential
issues
▪ Other
• Opportunity is independent and not linked
to other initiatives 10SUMMARY
▪ BOTB is longstanding cultural partner
▪ Facing significant financial and
operational pressures
▪ Staff have worked collaboratively to
explore solutions
• Pilot program approved by Board in 2024
and successfully implemented
• Second opportunity identified with
potential meaningful benefits
▪ Staff have considered the many

can be implemented successfully
11RECOMMENDATIONS
A. THAT the Vancouver Park Board approve a request by Bard on the Beach
Shakespeare Festival for the lease of space at Burrard Civic Marina land
storage lot for use as a temporary storage facility, including the installation of
a Quonset tent structure, as outlined in this report;
B. THAT the Vancouver Park Board authorize staff to negotiate, to the
satisfaction of the Park Board’s General Manager and the City’s Director of
Legal Services, and enter into an agreement with Bard on the Beach
Shakespeare Festival for the abovementioned initiative for a term of ten (10)
years; and
C. THAT no legal rights or obligations shall arise, and no consents, permissions,
or licenses are granted hereby, and none shall arise or be granted hereafter
unless and until all contemplated legal documentation has been executed and
delivered by all parties.
