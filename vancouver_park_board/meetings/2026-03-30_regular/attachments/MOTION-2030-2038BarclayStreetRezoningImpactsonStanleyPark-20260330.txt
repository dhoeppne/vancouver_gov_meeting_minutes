MOTION on NOTICE
Submitted at the Regular Park Board Meeting on February 2, 2026
2030–2038 Barclay Street Rezoning Impacts on Stanley Park
SUBMITTED BY: Commissioner Bastyovanszky
WHEREAS
1. The City of Vancouver is currently advancing a rezoning application for a large-
scale hotel redevelopment at 2030–2038 Barclay Street, proposing the
replacement of an existing low-rise residential building with a significantly
intensified commercial hotel use; and
2. The proposed redevelopment is located immediately adjacent to the western edge
of Stanley Park, raising potential implications for park access, traffic circulation,
emergency egress, pedestrian and cycling safety, environmental interface, and
heritage trees along the park boundary; and
3. Park Board planners have indicated that while feedback may be provided through
the City’s internal review process, the Park Board does not currently have a formal
public forum to review or discuss the impacts of adjacent non-park developments
on park lands and operations; and
4. Community members and local media have raised concerns regarding the scale
of the proposed hotel, increased congestion, neighbourhood impacts, and
precedent-setting development pressure at the edge of Stanley Park, including:
i. City rezoning application: large-scale hotel redevelopment at 2030–
2038 Barclay Street
ii. Community coverage and concerns:
iii. https://www.vancitylookout.com/p/west-end-residents-organize-
against-barclay-street-hotel
iv. https://www.thewestendjournal.ca/blog/2025/10/1/a-closer-look
v. https://stop2030barclay.ca/
vi. https://bcitnews.com/west-enders-fight-to-keep-high-rise-hotel-out/
5. As the elected body with statutory responsibility for the stewardship, protection,
and operation of Vancouver’s parks, the Park Board has a public interest in
understanding how adjacent developments may affect Stanley Park and
associated infrastructure.
Park Board Meeting: March 30, 2026THEREFORE BE IT RESOLVED
A. THAT the Vancouver Park Board request a formal briefing at a Committee meeting
on the proposed rezoning and hotel redevelopment at 2030–2038 Barclay Street;
B. THAT City of Vancouver staff from Planning, Engineering, and relevant
departments be requested to present on the potential impact on:
i. traffic flow affecting Stanley Park access points;
ii. emergency access and egress;
iii. the environment and heritage trees along the park boundary;
iv. pedestrian and cycling safety; and
v. park-adjacent infrastructure;
C. THAT staff report back on the scope of Park Board jurisdiction, consultation
authority, and potential safeguards available when major developments occur
immediately adjacent to park lands.
D. THAT the Park Board requests that City Council receive the report from Park Board
on the impact to Stanley Park prior to review the proposed redevelopment.
Park Board Meeting: March 30, 2026
