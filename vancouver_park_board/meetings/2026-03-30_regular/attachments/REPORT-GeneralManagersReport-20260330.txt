Celebrating Vancouver’s Cherry Trees: Festival Collaboration & Recent Plantings
The Park Operations Urban Forestry team is pleased to highlight ongoing collaboration with the
Vancouver Cherry Blossom Festival and recent specialty tree plantings in David Lam Park and
Queen Elizabeth Park.
At David Lam Park, the festival traditionally begins in the northwest corner with the flowering of
the Akebono cherry trees. The 2021 heat dome significantly affected this grove, resulting in the
decline of several trees. This year, the Team replaced six cherries with larger-caliper specimens
to provide immediate visual impact and ensure festival visitors could continue enjoying the iconic
blooms without interruption.
At Queen Elizabeth Park, a well-loved "Grandpa" cherry tree recently failed and required removal.
To maintain the park’s celebrated spring display, three new cherry trees were planted nearby. To
enhance species diversity and stagger the flowering period, the Shirotae variety—commonly
known as the Mt. Fuji cherry—was selected. This early-flowering ornamental tree produces
distinctive double white blossoms. The newly planted cherries can be found along the pathway
leading toward Bloedel Conservatory from the west parking lot, just north of the established
Akebono trees currently in bloom.
These plantings reinforce the Park Board's commitment to preserving Vancouver’s culturally
significant cherry displays while proactively managing the urban forest for resilience and public
enjoyment.
2026 Daisy Chin Woman of Inspiration Award
On March 5, staff at the Vancouver Board of Parks and Recreation gathered at Beach Avenue
and online to celebrate the 2026 Daisy Chin Woman of Inspiration Award.
This annual award reflects the Park Board’s commitment to fostering leadership and
representation within our workforce, ensuring that women are empowered to contribute
meaningfully both within our organization and in the broader community. We recognize and
celebrate the women on our team to foster an inclusive environment where women feel a strong
sense of belonging, opportunity, and empowerment.
The 2026 recipient is Katy Amon, a Senior Planner with the Planning and Policy team since 2016.
She is a tireless advocate for the Park Board’s mission, championing the preservation and growth
of our park spaces with unwavering dedication. Katy leads by example, acknowledging the
humanity in others while setting a high standard of excellence. Those who work with her feel
inspired and fortunate.
This year’s other panelists included Tina Fernandes - Project Manager II from Park Operations,
Rachael Greenfield – Office Support Clerk III from Park Operations, and Melanie Ware - Clerk III
from Recreation Services. 30 additional Park Board staff were also nominated by their peers.

March 31, 2026, marks the tenth anniversary of the passing of our colleague and Park Board
arborist, Jody Taylor, who was fatally injured while working in Connaught Park.
Jody, 43 at the time of his passing, was a beloved father, an avid photographer, and a hard-core
rock singer who loved life and lived it to the fullest. He was a valued member of our Park Board
family and is deeply missed. A memorial tree and a dedicated bench stand in Jody’s name near
the entrance to Stanley Park on Beach Avenue, serving as lasting reminders of his life and
legacy.
As we remember Jody, we are also reminded that safety is a shared responsibility. Each of us
has a role to play—by working safely, following established procedures, identifying and mitigating
hazards, and looking out for one another. Supporting our coworkers, speaking up when
something doesn’t feel right, and taking the time to do the job safely are essential parts of our
work.
Let Jody’s memory reinforce our commitment to safety so that we all go home at the end of the
day to our families, friends, and loved ones.
Council Business (relating to or impacting parks & recreation):
Council to receive a report on March 31, 2026, titled “Funding Application to Green and Inclusive
Community Buildings Program”, which would endorse a funding application for the Champlain
Heights Community Centre Energy Retrofit project for the amount of $2.3M.
Council to receive a report on April 1, 2026, titled “Contract Award for the Provision of a Recreation
Management System”, which would authorize City staff to negotiate an agreement for the
provision of a Recreation Management System for the Park Board with The Active Network.
Council to consider a motion on April 1, 2026, titled “Fostering Inclusive and Diverse Democracy
Through Harassment Prevention”, which would endorse the Elect Respect pledge and further
calls on other elected officials, candidates, elector organizations, and community members to
support the campaign.
Council to consider a motion on April 1, 2026, titled “Creating a City of Vancouver Festival Support
Fund”, which would direct staff to report back on the development of a fund designed to provide
stable, transparent, multi-year financial support for qualifying cultural festivals and major public
events.
Council to consider a motion on April 1, 2026, titled “Establishing a City Comptroller By-law for
Budget Efficiency and Asset Management Framework”, which would direct staff to report back on
a draft Comptroller By-law to establish a City Comptroller as an independent oversight officer
reporting directly to Council.
