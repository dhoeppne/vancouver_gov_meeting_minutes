2What are Affiliation and Allocation?
▪ Affiliation prioritizes access to facilities for groups based on public
benefit, inclusion, safety, and equity.
▪ Allocation decides how a limited number of spaces are strategically,
consistently, and equitably distributed among user groups.
• To implement permitting changes in 2027, these policies need to
be completed by July 2026.
3Project Drivers
▪ Historically, space was carried over for those who were there first.
Without policy guidelines, inequities and inefficiencies developed.
▪ Municipalities are shifting from historic allocation to prioritize
community, equity, safety, and alignment with sport standards.
▪ The Board approved Comprehensive Fee-setting
Framework connects taxpayer investment to services that provide
greatest public benefit.
▪ Staff developed a framework of allocation best practices in
collaboration with several western Canadian municipalities.
4Benefits and Impacted Groups
▪ Affiliation and Allocation Policies will improve experiences for user
groups accessing pools, arenas, and outdoor sport facilities.
o Established groups will find efficiencies in a clear, consistent, and
transparent process for booking a limited number of facilities.
o Emerging and equity deserving groups will see improved access
to spaces to grow their sports communities.
o Swimmers, divers, hockey players, figure skaters, soccer, rugby,
baseball, softball players, and many more, will benefit from
opportunities to play provided through policy guidance.
5Affiliation and Allocation and Public Good
▪ Recreation facilities are subsidized by taxpayer investment, with the
full costs of Capital and Operating higher than most fees charged.
▪ Affiliation and allocation prioritization connects access to these
facilities to the relative benefit provided to everyone in Vancouver.
▪ For e.g., prioritizing youth sports promotes lifelong healthy living
habits and physical literacy that benefits everyone:
o Decreased future health care costs;
o Reduced reliance on emergency services; and,
o Increased social well-being.
6Approach and Engagement
▪ Staff reviewed historic practices, researched precedents, and built
on established frameworks.
▪ Engagement with user groups shaped the direction and will be
essential moving forward:
• A survey was completed in 2025 to understand challenges,
with responses from nearly 125 organizations.
• A launch workshop was held in December 2025 with all user
groups invited (75 participated).
• In early 2026, engagement with user groups by service line
confirmed the criteria and principles shared in this update.
7Criteria for Affiliation
▪ 70%+ participants are residents.
▪ Registered non-profit.
▪ In good standing with governing body
and the Park Board.
▪ Committed to SafeSport.
▪ Provides mechanism that allows users
facing financial barriers to participate.

9Interim Aquatics Approach
▪ Ahead of the Vancouver Aquatic Centre closure, an interim affiliation
and allocation approach is being developed.
▪ Staff engaged groups in February 2026 and will return to discuss
allocation after completing pool capacity analysis.
▪ This interim aquatics approach is aligned with larger policy
development but advancing first due to necessity – it will inform and
then be replaced by full policies later, if approved by the Board.

11Project Dependencies
▪ A series of connected projects depend on completion of Affiliation
and Allocation Policies:
• Approved policies are necessary to implement software changes.
• Improvements to user fee policies are also underway.
• The combination of updated policies allows for enhanced user
experience and increased equity in permitting for 2027.
▪ A lack of policy leads to user group needs not being met, increased
inequities, and sets us back from regional and provincial direction.

communities.
▪ Staff will consult Provincial Sport
